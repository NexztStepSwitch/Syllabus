# 15 — Soft Skills & Interview Prep

> **Module rating:** ★★★★★ (Mandatory) · **Difficulty:** Foundation · **Est. effort:** ongoing (2–3 h/week) · **Depth target:** Interview level
> **Prerequisites:** none — run this *throughout*, not at the end.

## Why this module

As coding got easier to game with AI, **behavioral and design rounds carry more of the hiring decision**. At 3.5 YOE you're evaluated on **ownership, communication, and judgment**, not just correctness. Strong candidates *narrate trade-offs*; average ones go silent and code.

---

## Core

### Behavioral / STAR — ★★★★★ · Foundation · ongoing
- **What exactly:** STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) with **measurable results**; 6–8 stories covering ownership, conflict, failure/learning, leadership, ambiguity, impact. Map to Amazon Leadership Principles (they weave LPs into technical rounds).
- **Interview Qs:** "tell me about a time you disagreed with a decision"; "a project that failed"; "when you took ownership beyond your role."
- **Red flags:** "we" with no "I"; no metrics; rambling; blaming others.

### Communicating trade-offs — ★★★★★ · Foundation · ongoing
- **Why:** the #1 differentiator in design rounds. Think out loud; state assumptions; give the decision *and what you gave up*.
- **Practice:** for every design/LLD, end with "I chose X over Y because…, accepting the cost of…".
- **2026 note (AI-collaboration signal):** if AI is allowed, verbalize what you delegate vs verify and how you catch the model's mistakes.

### Resume & project storytelling — ★★★★★ · Foundation · 1 day + updates
- **What exactly:** impact-first bullets (action + tech + measurable outcome), one strong end-to-end project you can defend deeply, tailoring to the role.
- **Red flags:** responsibility lists with no impact; tech you can't explain; unquantified claims.
- **Milestone tie-in:** update your resume at the timeline checkpoints ([`18_learning_timeline.md`](18_learning_timeline.md)).

### Mock interviews — ★★★★★ · Foundation · weekly
- **What exactly:** timed coding, LLD, HLD, and behavioral mocks with a partner/platform; record + review; **confirm each company's format + AI policy with the recruiter** ([MIXED]).

### Negotiation & levelling — ★★★★☆ · Foundation · 0.5 day
- **What exactly:** know the level (SDE-2/mid), comp components, competing offers, don't disclose numbers first; understand 2/3.5/5+ YOE expectations to target the right level.

## Company-format cheatsheet

| Company type | Emphasis |
|--------------|----------|
| Google/OpenAI | classic DSA + design (DB selection) |
| Meta | AI-enabled coding round [MIXED]; design + behavioral |
| Anthropic | no AI tools in interview |
| Amazon | Leadership Principles woven in; behavioral weight high |
| Netflix/Stripe | open-ended design, domain depth |
| Razorpay/PhonePe/Swiggy | LLD + HLD + machine coding + DSA + HM; concurrency-heavy |
| Fintech (Stripe/Wise/JPMC/Goldman) | idempotency, consistency, correctness, auditability |

## Hands-on / practice

- **Lab 1:** write 6–8 STAR stories; get feedback; trim each to ~2 min.
- **Lab 2:** do 1 recorded design mock/week; review where you went silent or skipped trade-offs.
- **Lab 3:** rewrite 3 resume bullets to impact-first with metrics.
- **Scenario:** interviewer changes constraints mid-design ("now it must be strongly consistent") — practice adapting calmly.

---

## Checklists

### Interview Checklist
- **Must know:** 6–8 STAR stories with metrics; trade-off narration habit; a deeply-defensible project; the company's format + AI policy.
- **Good to know:** LP mapping; levelling expectations; negotiation basics.
- **Bonus:** AI-collaboration narration; handling hostile follow-ups.

### Hands-on Checklist
- **Must:** ran ≥4 mocks across coding/LLD/HLD/behavioral; polished resume.
- **Good:** recorded + reviewed mocks; confirmed formats with recruiters.
- **Bonus:** negotiated with a competing offer.

## Detailed Syllabus (topic checklist)

> Full topic inventory for reference. Priorities/depth are in the guide above.

**1. Communication**
- Explaining technical concepts clearly; storytelling with projects; structured verbal reasoning (think out loud)

**2. Problem-solving approach**
- Breaking down requirements; clarifying assumptions before coding/designing; communicating trade-offs explicitly

**3. Behavioral interviews**
- STAR method; project deep-dives; strengths/weaknesses; conflict resolution; leadership/ownership; failure & learning stories
- Company-specific leadership principles (e.g., Amazon LP) awareness

**4. Collaboration & teamwork**
- Agile/Scrum participation; code-review etiquette (giving & receiving); pairing; mentoring

**5. Interview strategy**
- Structuring answers; whiteboard/coding communication; asking the interviewer good questions
- Coding interview approach (clarify → examples → approach → code → test)
- System design delivery (drive the conversation, state assumptions)

**6. Resume & positioning**
- Impact-oriented bullets (metrics); project narratives; tailoring to role/level (2 / 3.5 / 5+ YOE)

**7. Logistics**
- Company interview formats cheatsheet; mock interviews; salary negotiation basics; offer evaluation

**8. Checklist**
- Be concise but clear; show reasoning not just answers; demonstrate growth mindset & ownership
